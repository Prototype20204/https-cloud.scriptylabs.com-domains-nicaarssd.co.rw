About Us

Nile Institute for Climate Adaptation and Agricultural Research (NICAAR) is a South Sudan-based private agricultural, rural and development, environmental, and natural resources research institute. Founded in December 2023, the center is registered by South Sudan’s registrar of companies under register number…………. NICAAR’s head office is currently in Juba, the capital of South Sudan. NICAAR’s research programmes and activities are devloped based on the the government of the Republic of South Sudan’s priorities that are guided by the Country Progrmming Framwork (CPF), the Comprehensive Agriculture Master Plan (CAMP) and other existing relevant government policy documents in the line ministries. NICAAR’s programs and research activities are also aligned with and guided by the sustainable development goals (SDGs) 1, 2, 3 and 13 as described in the mission statement below. Implementation of NICAAR’s research programs and activities extend to state levels, especially the counties that are situated along the Nile not only in South Sudan, but also in the Nile Basin region. Anchored on its core values and mission statement, NICAAR’s vision is to be a leading private agricultural and rural development, knowledge management, and climate change adaptation research center in South Sudan and in the Nile Basin region, with an ultimate goal of “economic growth and enhance sustainable livelihoods and agri-food systems for both urban and rural populations not only in South Sudan but also in the Nile Basin countries through research in climate change adaptation, agriculture and environment” For efficient and effective service delivery to the target populations in South Sudan and beyond in terms of research, consultancy, and training, NICAAR collaborates with other research institutions and humanitarian organizations which include the Capacity Development and Training Initiative (CDTI) as a national non-governmental Organisation (NGO) with research and training focus in the thematic areas, specialized United Nations (UN) agencies in the thematic research areas as well as international research and training institutions, national and international non-governmental organizations (NNGOs/INGOs) in the Country.

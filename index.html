MESSAGE FROM THE CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER (CEO)

Triggered by the recent national debate on the proposed River Nile dredging initiatives in South Sudan in the first quarter of the year 2023, coupled with protracted climate change crises on agriculture and food security globally and in the sub-Saharan Africa region; and motivated by personal professional training, research experience, and a strong conviction and desire to contribute to the body of knowledge to enhance national development through research, training, and consultancy to the people of South Sudan and in the Nile Basin Countries, it is in the backdrop of these desires that led to the conception, inception and founding of the Nile Institute of Climate Adaptation and Agricultural Research (NICAAR). As part of its development agenda, NBRI will join hands together with other research institutions both in South Sudan and the region to advance research and knowledge creation in line with the government of the Republic of South Sudan’s priorities. As mentioned earlier, NICAAR’s research focus will be guided by three main sustainable development goals which include; SDG number 1: No poverty: End extreme poverty in all forms by 2030, SDG number 2: End hunger: Achieve food security and improved nutrition and promote sustainable agriculture, SDG 3; “Good Health and Well-being: Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages” and SDG number 13: Climate Action: Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts. NICAAR’s strategies to attain its desired goal include; technical Cooperation, partnership, and Networking with other research institutions - both private and public institutions of higher learning such as colleges and universities not only in South Sudan but also in the region and globally. Needs-based and data-driven research programming and resource mobilization, knowledge generation, management, and sharing, capacity development and strengthening, research, training and consultancy focusing on crops, livestock, and fisheries production, climate change adaptation and mitigation, and environmental management by investing in Environmental and Social Impact Assessment (ESIA) processes among others. By doing this it is our expectation as NICAAR team that South Sudan will be able to make use of its untapped natural resources for sustainable food security, agri-food systems and climate change early warning systems.
